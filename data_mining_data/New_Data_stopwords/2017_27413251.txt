 Background. Colorectal cancer (CRC) second deadliest malignancy worldwide. study aimed compare diagnostic accuracy different fecal markers detection colorectal adenomas cancer. Methods. Stool samples patients referred colonoscopy collected analysis tumor M2 pyruvate kinase (M2PK), human hemoglobin (Hb), hemoglobin/haptoglobin (Hb/Hp) complex, fecal calprotectin (FC), matrix metalloproteinase-9 (MMP-9). Results. Sensitivity specificity M2PK adenomas sized > 1 cm 60% 67.5% CRC 94.7% 67.5%. Sensitivity specificity iFOBT adenomas sized >/= 1 cm 80% 72.5% CRC 94.7% 72.5%. Sensitivity specificity Hb/Hp complex adenomas sized >/= 1 cm 80% 52.9% CRC 100% 52.9%. Sensitivity FC MMP-9 CRC 77.8% 72.2%. Combined use M2PK, iFOBT, FC resulted sensitivity specificity 95% 47.5% detection adenomas sized >/= 1 cm. Discussion. CRC, sensitivity M2PK, iFOBT, Hb/Hp complex proved high. Combined use M2PK, iFOBT, FC may valuable detection large adenomas.
